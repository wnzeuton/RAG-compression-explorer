Title: 2031-2048 Timeline
Type: Timeline

Legal uncertainty surrounded cross-border AI systems. Governments struggled with jurisdiction. Thomas Albrecht joined regulatory working groups. His focus was automation accountability.

2032
International AI liability frameworks were drafted. Albrecht emphasized legal clarity and responsibility. The frameworks addressed autonomous decision harms. Adoption varied by region.

2034
Scholarly debate intensified around sovereign governance in automated economies. Albrecht published influential legal analyses. His work shaped academic and policy discussions. Universities incorporated his writings.

2037

2048
AI governance frameworks matured internationally. Albrecht was regarded as a leading authority. Legal standards stabilized around transparency and accountability. His work defined long-term regulatory norms.
